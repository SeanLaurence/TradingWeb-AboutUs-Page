Glass Beads

GENERAL USE AND PRECAUTIONARY INFORMATION

Industrial glass beads are commonly used for blast cleaning, surface finishing, deburring, peening, and producing smooth, uniform finishes on metal, plastic, and glass components across industries such as aerospace, automotive, metalworking, and electronics. They are also used in reflective road markings and as fillers in various industrial products. When handling or using glass beads, workers should wear appropriate PPE—including goggles, gloves, and respirators—and ensure proper ventilation to avoid inhaling fine dust. Equipment should be operated at the correct pressure to prevent surface damage or bead breakage, and machines must be regularly maintained. Glass beads should be stored in a dry place to prevent moisture clumping, and any waste or spent abrasive material must be collected and disposed of according to local regulations.

Steel Grit

GENERAL USE AND PRECAUTIONARY INFORMATION

Industrial steel grit is used for heavy-duty blast cleaning, surface preparation, and removing rust or coatings in industries like shipbuilding, metal fabrication, and foundries. It delivers strong cutting action and creates a good profile for coatings. When using steel grit, operators should wear proper PPE, ensure good ventilation, and avoid inhaling dust. Equipment should be properly calibrated and maintained, and the material should be stored dry to prevent corrosion. Used grit and dust must be collected and disposed of according to safety and environmental regulations.
